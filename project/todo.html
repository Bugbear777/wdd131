Sedona Trolley – A Scenic and Story-Filled Ride
Established in 1993, the Sedona Trolley offers fun, informative, and affordable guided tours through Sedona's stunning red rock landscapes. It's affectionately known among locals and visitors as "the best first thing to do in Sedona." The nostalgic open-air trolleys—reminiscent of San Francisco’s classic streetcars—let passengers enjoy the fresh air while taking in breathtaking vistas.

Red Rock Hiking
Sedona’s hiking trails offer an unparalleled mix of stunning scenery and diverse terrain, winding through vibrant red rock formations, shady pine groves, and high desert plateaus. Popular routes like Cathedral Rock Trail and Bell Rock Pathway provide breathtaking panoramic views, while Devil’s Bridge rewards hikers with a dramatic natural sandstone arch. For those seeking a quieter escape, trails such as Boynton Canyon and West Fork lead through serene canyons and along trickling creeks, especially beautiful in autumn when the foliage turns golden. With options ranging from easy strolls to challenging climbs, Sedona’s trail network caters to all skill levels, promising both adventure and moments of peaceful connection with nature.
